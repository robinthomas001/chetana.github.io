Guna is the gateway of Malwa and Chambal. Guna is a fast developing city , with factories and other business institutions. The population of Guna is approximately 1666767, in the area of 6484.63 sq.km according to 2001 census survey.

Guna being one of the industrial cities of the state of Madhya Pradesh and populated district has increased number of migrant workers and slum - dwellers. The high population and minimum living conditions has given birth to lot of evils and health related problems. HIV infected population, sex trade; trafficking , malnutrition and tuberculosis, illiterate youth vulnerable to social evils. Disabilities are the hall mark of this district.

Approach

PLWHA not only need help in their personal life but also necessary to involve and support the whole family in the programs. Most of the PLWHA used to be the sole bread winner of the family and the prolonged illness has drained out all the family resources. Therefore, the need of the whole family has to be taken in to consideration while designing any care and support program for the PLWHA. CHETANA adopts a holistic approach to address the HIV/AIDS situation.

Treatment & Nutrition

Travelling allowances to the PLWHA to go to ART centres at Indore , Bhopal and Gwalior which are 300, 250 and 200 kms away from Guna respectively.

Counselling and nutritional support help PLWHA to derive the desired benefit out of the ART .

Free medical consultation and medicines to PLWHA by way of outpatient clinic to treat and prevent opportunistic infections.

Counselling & family visits

Immediate personal counselling is required to help the PLWHA accept their situation and to educate them on methods of positive living.

The project provides counselling to family members to reduce stigma and to integrate the PLWHA back into the family and society.

Awareness, disease management, care and support, behavioral change, positive living are some of the common issues on which the PLWHA, their peers and family members need counselling.

Read More

Awareness Generation

Project carries out extensive awareness programs using different media, especially street plays , leaflets celebration of remarkable days etc.

The project also concentrates on different target groups like Auto rickshaw drivers , truck drivers, sex workers, eunuchs, migrated population and conduct health camps along with Elisa test.

Giving awareness education to the PLWHA about HIV/AIDS, precautions, drug adherence and positive living are the most important in reducing HIV transmission.

Education

The project is supporting children of PLWHA who are unable to send their children to school for want of resources for all their educational needs. While the education support would increase the level of awareness and reduce stigma in the family, it would, in the long run, prepare the next generation for a better life.

The education programme of the project would extend to equip the children for job by providing job oriented training after secondary/ higher secondary school level.

Capacity building

Self-employment of the individual mainly through capacity building is one of the main objectives of the project. The project personal as well as the PLWHA regularly attend various programs, which broaden their horizons and empower them.

Income generating activities

The project encourages and help PLWHA who regain their health as a result of medication and nutrition to engage in small Income Generating Activity (IGA) in order to promote a sense of dignity and self- worth among them.

Mutual support groups

In order to ensure peer support, the project from PLWHA into small groups on the basis of proximity of residence. The family members of the PLWHA will also be involved in these groups so that support can be ensured in case of need. This Mutual Support Groups (MSG) will provide peer counselling and also carry out need assessment.

CHIRAG - Education and rehabilitation programe

We extent our support to the people of various categories. The poverty and difficulties of the parents to educate their physically disabled children, the insecurity and inferiority complex developed by the disabled children and their desire to be educated and to live at their own cost made us to respond to this silent call. As a result we have 'CHIRAG' program educating children among whom there are physically disabled and children affected and infected with HIV. While the education support would increase the level of awareness and reduce stigma in the family, it would, in the long run, prepare the next generation for a better life.

Udaay - Rehabilitation of children

The living conditions, locality and family practices influences the children and the play vital role in their life and carrier. Considering this reality we planned to provide residential education in our boarding to the children of the commercial sex workers. It takes a long time and effort to change the practices of the commercial workers, but what we can do is to provide better education and exposure to better possibilities where by the children can dream higher and find a carrier which is mostly expected and appreciated by the society. These children can also become instruments of change in the family and society. They are means for us to reach to the parents with other options of life and means of safety in their life.

Axshaya - Tuberculosis Eradication program

The state and central government has taken various measures to detect and to prevent treatment to those who suffer from tuberculosis. Chetana having its aim of achieving total health and dignified living of all people with basic education and professional training is in tie up with the local government and Catholic Health Association of India to promote health and to eradicate tuberculosis.

SATHI - Guidance and support to the auto drivers

SATHI promotes dignified living and dedicated service of the autorickshaw drivers and recognition and due respect to them from the society. Sathi means friend. We envisage that every auto driver must be friend and guide to the travelers who approach them. They will be guide to the strangers who visit the city. Their families will gather together once a month which promotes the development of the family members, their entertainment and cultural gathering. They will be also encouraged to savings.

Monitoring & Evaluation

The project adopts a very transparent method of evaluation. A detailed time- bound action plan along with the budget is prepared before the beginning of the Financial Year: Regular monitoring of the various programs under the project is done by way of weekly, monthly reviews within the project team and quarterly reviews and annual evaluation with the help of an external facilitator: Representatives of benefactors, beneficiaries, government agencies and managing committee of the project also actively participate in the annual evaluation.
